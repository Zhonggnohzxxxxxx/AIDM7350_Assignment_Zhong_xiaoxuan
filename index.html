THE SILK ROAD

The Concept And Background Of The Silk Road, March 20, 2025

The Silk Road generally refers to the overland Silk Road. Broadly speaking, it is further divided into the overland Silk Road and the Maritime Silk Road. The Silk Road was not only a commercial thoroughfare for ancient Asia and Europe to exchange what they had for what they lacked but also a road of friendship that promoted friendly exchanges between Asian and European countries and China and facilitated cultural communication between the East and the West.

EACH FLOWER IS A UNIQUE UNIVERSE

A Cultural Relic Tells A Silk Road Story, March 20, 2025

In ancient times, mountains, rivers, deserts, seas... often confined humanity to a relatively enclosed geographical environment, but never hindered the deep longing in the hearts of humans: to communicate.

There once was a road that crossed mountains and deserts, breaking through geographical constraints, connecting ancient China, neighboring countries, and the West. The sound of camel bells echoed as caravans loaded with silk, porcelain, wool, jade, and other goods traveled busily along this route; monks and envoys pursued their dreams and hurried along this path. This road became a major channel for commercial and cultural exchanges between the East and the West.

This route was named the Silk Road due to the representative silk exported from China. Although silk is used in its name, the exchanges on this road were not limited to silk and silk weaving skills; it was the main channel for cultural exchanges between the East and the West. On this road, what was exchanged included both materials and commodities, as well as technologies and cultures. These commodities and technologies formed the main content of mutual learning and mutual appreciation on the Silk Road.

THE NEW SILK ROAD

The New Silk Road Economic Belt, stretching from the Asia-Pacific Economic Circle in the east to the developed European Economic Circle in the west, is considered as "the longest and most developmentally promising economic corridor in the world." The overland Silk Road Economic Belt connects the vibrant Asia-Pacific region in the east, passes through the resource-rich Central Asia in the middle, and leads to developed European economies in the west. Meanwhile, the Maritime Silk Road links coastal port cities between China and Southeast Asian countries, and through maritime interconnectivity, port city cooperation mechanisms, and marine economic cooperation, it ultimately forms a Maritime Silk Road Economic Belt. This will not only benefit China and ASEAN but also radiate out to South Asia and the Middle East.
